Sources & methodology

Rent (3-bed, mid) — verified. NYC StreetEasy (3+BR median $4,995; outer mid $4,800); Chicago RentCafe/Yardi ($3,768); Paris OLAP official observatory (€26.6/m² → T4 €2,000); Toronto TRREB (C$3,757); London ONS PIPR by borough (£2,500). Buy mode adds mortgage + property tax + ~3%/yr appreciation as equity.

Childcare under 3 — triangulated. NYC ~$20k (Winnie/ChildCarePath); Chicago ~$16.5k; Paris ~€6k (CAF scale); Toronto ~C$5.5k (City of Toronto / CWELCC $22-a-day cap); London ~£12k net (Coram 2026 + funded hours).

Effective tax. Real 2025–26 brackets plus the deductions people take: US standard deduction + child tax credit + dependent-care FSA; France quotient familial + 10% frais; Canada BPA + CPP/EI + child benefit; UK personal allowance + pension salary-sacrifice. Output is the rate actually paid, not the headline.

Employer-side comp. France employer social charges ≈ 43% of gross (OECD Taxing Wages 2026; URSSAF). US employer health ≈ $20k/yr family (KFF 2025) + 401(k). UK employer NI ~13.8%. Real compensation, off-payslip.

Health as expected value. ~88% normal year + ~12% high-cost year, probability-weighted (US: KFF 2025 premiums/deductible/OOP-max; Europe: Sécu+mutuelle / OHIP / NHS, low tail).

Coverage & taxes. 12 cities across 8 tax systems: US (IRS 2025 + state: NY, IL, and no-tax TX/FL/WA per Tax Foundation), France (DGFiP + quotient familial), Germany (§32a EStG + ~20% social, Ehegattensplitting), Canada (CRA + Ontario/BC), UK (HMRC + NI), Japan (national + 10% local, OECD/PwC), Australia (ATO + 2% Medicare), Singapore (IRAS + CPF). Effective rates, not headline.

Salaries. levels.fyi (tech), US BLS OES & Canada Job Bank (government), cross-checked vs StatCan median household income. Per-city multipliers reflect real same-occupation gaps.

Purchasing power. OECD comparative price levels (US=100) convert nominal disposable income into what it actually buys locally.

Limitations. Rounded illustrative estimates, not advice. Groceries, transport and insurance remain representative averages (estimate-grade). Inflation, pay growth and appreciation use steady assumed rates.
